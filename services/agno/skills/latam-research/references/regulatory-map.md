# Regulatory Map — Data Protection & Health in Latin America

## Data Protection Laws (relevant for all 3 products)

| Country | Law | Enforcement | Key Requirement |
|---------|-----|-------------|-----------------|
| Colombia | Ley 1581/2012 + Decreto 1377/2013 | SIC | Explicit consent, data localization optional |
| Mexico | LFPDPPP (2010) | INAI | Privacy notice required, consent for sensitive data |
| Brazil | LGPD (2020) | ANPD | Similar to GDPR, DPO required for large processors |
| Argentina | Ley 25.326 (2000, updating) | AAIP | EU adequacy status, strong individual rights |
| Chile | Ley 19.628 (updating to new law 2024+) | Pending new authority | Currently weak enforcement, new law strengthens it |
| Peru | Ley 29733 (2011) | APDP | Consent required, cross-border transfer restrictions |

## Health Data Regulations (relevant for Docflow)

| Country | Law | Key Requirement |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| Colombia | Resolución 1995/1999 + Ley 23/1981 | Clinical history is property of patient, 10yr retention |
| Mexico | NOM-024-SSA3-2012 | Electronic health records standard |
| Brazil | CFM Resolution 1821/2007 | Digital medical records, 20yr retention |
| Argentina | Ley 26.529 (2009) | Patient rights to medical records |

## WhatsApp Business Regulations (relevant for Whabi)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Meta Business Verification | Required for API access |
| Message Templates | Must be pre-approved by Meta |
| 24-hour Window | Free-form messages only within 24h of last customer message |
| Opt-in Required | Customer must consent to receive messages |
| Business Hours | Some countries restrict commercial messaging hours |
